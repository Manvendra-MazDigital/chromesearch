I'm Louis Gray with Google Developers Live, and welcome to the second edition of Google Developers Weekly — our highlight reel of what's happening in the world of Google Developers on the set and off. Last week we kicked off this show, keeping you on top of all of the events in our studios worldwide. Let's go to the tape and see this week's highlights, and preview the week to come. Google Drive and Google+ Photos have always been good friends, but it's now time to take that relationship to a brand new level. Ali Ashar took to the studio to show us how photos in Google Drive are now integrated with your Google+ account.

Also in the world of Google+, Joanna Smith invited product managers Tor and Joelle to discuss the launch of the Google+ domain's API, enabling companies and enterprises to develop tools for their businesses on the Google+ platform. Every single week, our team from Tel Aviv takes a deep dive into the world of APIs or host experts from the world of web and mobile. This Wednesday, Ido Green showed how you can leverage the power of NodeJS on Google Compute Engine to pull information from the Yahoo Finance API, and work with that data in Google Docs.

This is just another cool example of how open standards can help different company systems work together. Back here in Mountain View, Jeremy Walker of the YouTube team invited guest Vidyard into the studio. If you create content on YouTube, Vidyard can help you turn your video content into powerful marketing tools. And after all, don't engineers and developers always love marketing? The week in the studio wrapped with the latest entry in our Root Access series of growing your start up and navigating the world of venture capital. Don Dodge and Martin Omander updated us on the biggest trends from two of the hottest incubators at Silicon Valley — 500 Startups and Y Combinator. Also, this next week promises to be huge.

Brian Fitzpatrick, better known as "Fitz," will be taking on three big topics on GDL. First, organizational manipulation. Then data liberation. And finally, Google's Transparency Report. I'll be fully transparent with you. I'm looking forward to asking him the tough questions on business politics, and just how I can get all my data out of Google if I want to. Here on GDL, we may not have Shark Week, but we do have Fitz week. Outside of the studio, Google Analytics' Nick Mihailovski introduced a new analytics metadata API. Now you can find out which API and which metrics can be queried from Google Analytics core reporting.

Check out our Google Developers blog for more details. On our Google+ page, we shared a post about the new Dart Editor, which adds new features and fixes. The flashiest features, you can now inspect classes in running apps. Find out more at dartling.org/editor.
